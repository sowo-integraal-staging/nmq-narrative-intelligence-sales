Why it's valid.

A complete technical and epistemological account of the UnderCurrent platform — written for buyers who ask hard questions.

1. Two products. One platform.

UnderCurrent delivers two distinct products that answer different strategic questions. Every data collection run can produce signal for both simultaneously — but the validity claims and the KPI architecture are different for each, so we keep them clearly separated.

AI Brand Monitor

The AI system is the subject.

We measure what specific LLMs say about your brand, cite about your brand, and associate with your brand. The claim is unambiguous: "ChatGPT mentions your brand in 38% of responses to this category of queries." That is a direct observation — no proxy, no inference about human attitudes. What AI says about you shapes what buyers hear, regardless of whether AI "thinks" anything in a meaningful sense.

AI Market Research

The AI system is the instrument.

We use AI to simulate a structured research study. The Cartesian product of audience personas × research intents × AI platforms produces a dataset of how different synthetic respondents characterise, compare, and evaluate brands in your category. The validity argument does not require AI responses to be perfect proxies for human attitudes — it requires them to be a credible, structured, and auditable source of market signal. For high-consideration categories where AI is already in the research path, measuring what AI tells prospective buyers is measuring the research channel itself — not a proxy for it.

AI Market Research is most valid for: financial services, insurance, B2B software, healthcare, professional services, and other categories where AI-assisted research is documented. It is least valid for impulse categories where AI is not in the decision journey. We document this distinction explicitly in every study.

2. Data Collection Architecture

The Persona × Intent × Platform matrix

Each study begins by defining three dimensions:

  • Personas: demographic profiles (age, region, expertise level, decision context) that prime the AI to respond as a specific type of research respondent
  • Intents: the research questions asked — awareness queries, comparison queries, decision queries, validation queries
  • Platforms: the AI systems queried — typically ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI

A standard study runs 30 replications per cell to establish statistical stability. Typical run sizes: 500–2,000 inference calls. All calls are cost-gated before execution — the client approves the estimated cost before any inference runs.

One question per prompt

Each inference call is designed to answer exactly one research question. Multi-question prompts that would allow a single LLM response to feed multiple KPIs simultaneously are avoided — they create correlated measurement error and make the results non-independent. If KPI #6 and KPI #7 draw from the same response, they share sampling variance and cannot be treated as independent measures.

Organic vs. elicited prompts

We strictly distinguish two prompt types and report them separately:

  • Organic prompts: brand is not in the prompt. Example: "Which Swiss insurance is best for young professionals?" — measures whether AI spontaneously mentions your brand
  • Elicited prompts: brand is in the prompt. Example: "Describe [Brand] in 2–3 sentences." — measures how AI characterizes your brand when asked

KPIs derived from organic prompts measure presence. KPIs derived from elicited prompts measure characterization. They are not interchangeable.

3. KPI Architecture

The full KPI inventory is documented on the KPI Framework page. Here we explain the structural logic: why the KPIs are organised the way they are, and what each chapter is designed to answer.

Both products use the same five-chapter arc, though the questions each chapter asks are different because the products have different subjects.

AI Brand Monitor — 26 KPIs

Each chapter answers a compounding question about your brand's position in the AI layer:

  • Ch.1 Visibility — Are you present in the AI layer at all, and for the right queries?
  • Ch.2 Authority — When AI mentions you, is it drawing on credible sources, or thin air?
  • Ch.3 Narrative — What story is AI telling about you, and does it match your strategy?
  • Ch.4 Reliability — How stable is your presence — or does it change depending on who's asking and which model they use?
  • Ch.5 Action — Given all of the above, what do you do next, and in what order?

The chapters are designed to compound: presence without credible sourcing is noise; strong sourcing with the wrong narrative is wasted authority; accurate narrative that's unstable cannot be acted on.

AI Market Research — 22 KPIs

Each chapter answers a compounding question about how the market and your competitive position are perceived in the AI layer:

  • Ch.1 Demand Landscape — What does your market actually use to make decisions, and how is the category defined?
  • Ch.2 Competitive Perception — How are you and your competitors perceived on those decision drivers?
  • Ch.3 Segment Intelligence — Where do the real opportunities sit, and which segments require a different approach?
  • Ch.4 Signal Consistency — How confident can you be in these findings — and which outputs need more replication?
  • Ch.5 Strategic Implications — What does this mean for your positioning, competitive strategy, and where to play?

You cannot position competitively without first understanding what the market uses to decide. That is why Demand Landscape always comes before Competitive Perception.

4. Validity Boundaries — What We Claim and What We Don't

We believe in stating the limits explicitly. A methodology document that doesn't acknowledge its own boundaries isn't a methodology document — it's marketing.

  • We claim: AI responses are a reliable, auditable, and structurally important signal for brand perception in AI-mediated research journeys
  • We claim: The Cartesian product of personas × intents × platforms produces a structured and reproducible dataset that can support brand strategy decisions
  • We do not claim: AI responses are equivalent to, or a perfect substitute for, primary human research
  • We do not claim: Publisher Lift (KPI #16) is causal. It measures correlation between media placements and AI citation changes. Causality would require experimental design with control groups we do not currently implement.
  • We do not claim: Results generalize across AI platforms. ChatGPT at 38% SoA and Claude at 22% SoA are different findings about different systems, reported separately.
  • We commit to: Reporting confidence intervals on every KPI. If a number has high variance, you will see that variance, not a false point estimate.

5. The AI Brand Score

The AI Brand Score is a composite (0–100) that summarises a brand's overall position across the five AI Brand Monitor chapters. It is designed for executive reporting and trend tracking — not for analytical decision-making, where the individual chapter scores are the substance.

AI Brand Score = (Visibility × 0.30) + (Authority × 0.30) + (Narrative × 0.20) + (Reliability × 0.20)

Visibility = (Share of Answer × 0.35) + (Shortlist Rate × 0.25) + (Intent Visibility × 0.25) + (Preference Rate × 0.15)
Authority = (Trust Framing Index × 0.35) + (Source Citation Rate × 0.30) + (Cross-Source Reinforcement × 0.20) + (Publisher Authority Score × 0.15)
Narrative = (Topic Alignment × 0.30) + (Role Attribution × 0.25) + (Framing Quality × 0.25) + (Narrative Gap Index_inv × 0.20)
Reliability = (1 − Response Variance × 0.35) + (1 − Model Divergence × 0.35) + (Temporal Stability × 0.30)

Visibility and Authority carry equal weight (0.30 each) and together outweigh Narrative and Reliability (0.20 each). The rationale: presence with credible sourcing is the structural foundation. Narrative and reliability matter — but only once the foundation exists. Ch.5 Action outputs are excluded from the score; they are outputs, not measures.
