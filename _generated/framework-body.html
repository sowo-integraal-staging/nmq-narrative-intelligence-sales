Ch. 1
Visibility
“Are you in the room when buyers ask?”
Share of Answer
The proportion of relevant AI inference trials in which the brand is mentioned at least once, regardless of position, expressed as a percentage. Measured per cohort cell (persona × intent × model).
Shortlist Rate
The proportion of relevant inference trials in which the brand appears in the top 3 positions of a ranked AI response. Only applicable to prompts that elicit a ranked list.
Intent Visibility
SoA broken down by intent type (informational / comparative / buy-decision / navigational). Each intent type is scored separately; the composite is an intent-weighted average where buy-decision carries highest weight.
Share of Voice
The brand's mention count as a proportion of total brand mentions across all competitors in the defined competitive set, within the same inference batch.
Preference Rate
The proportion of inference trials using a forced-choice or top-recommendation prompt in which the brand is selected as the primary (first or only) recommendation.
Model Coverage
The number of major LLMs in which the brand achieves SoA above a defined threshold (default: 40%), expressed as a fraction of models tested.
Ch. 2
Authority
“Why does AI trust you — and who vouches for you?”
Source Citation Rate
The proportion of inference trials in which the model cites at least one identifiable external source (URL, publication name, or domain) in the same response that mentions the brand.
Publisher Citation Share
For all citation URLs extracted in trials where the brand is mentioned, the distribution of citations across identified publishers, expressed as a percentage share per outlet.
Publisher Authority Score
A weighted average of citations received, where each citation is weighted by the authority tier of the citing publisher (Tier 1 = 1.0, Tier 2 = 0.6, Tier 3 = 0.3). Normalised to 0–100.
Cross-Source Reinforcement Index
The proportion of brand citation clusters (per query) in which at least 2 distinct Tier 1 or Tier 2 publishers are cited together. Measures co-citation diversity, not just volume.
Trust Framing Index
A scored classification of the framing language surrounding brand mentions, on a four-point scale: Positive (1.0), Neutral (0.5), Qualified (0.25), Dismissive (0.0). Aggregated as a weighted mean.
Ch. 3
Narrative
“What story does AI actually tell about you?”
Role Attribution
The dominant role category assigned to the brand across inference trials, derived from keyword and framing classification of brand mention contexts. Reported as a distribution across role categories.
Topic Alignment Score
The proportion of brand mentions that occur within the client-defined set of target topics and intent types, versus off-target topics.
Reason-for-Recommendation
A ranked distribution of the attributes cited as reasons for recommending the brand, extracted and clustered from recommendation-context text.
Narrative Gap Index
A composite distance score between the client-declared desired positioning (attributes, role, topics) and the measured AI narrative (from Role Attribution, Topic Alignment, Reason-for-Recommendation). Normalised to 0–100 where 0 = perfect alignment, 100 = maximum divergence.
Framing Quality
A four-category classification of the predominant language framing surrounding brand mentions: Positive / Neutral / Qualified / Dismissive. Reported as a distribution; summary score is proportion of Positive + Neutral frames.
Comparative Narrative Role
The dominant competitive framing of the brand in responses that mention both the brand and at least one competitor. Categories: Leader / Challenger / Alternative / Complementary / Unclassified.
Ch. 4
Reliability
“Can you trust these numbers — and will they hold tomorrow?”
Response Variance
The standard deviation of SoA scores across all replications of the same prompt × cohort cell. Reported per KPI to provide a confidence band around each chapter's outputs.
Model Divergence Index
A measure of how much SoA scores vary across major models for the same prompt and cohort. Computed as the range (max − min) of per-model SoA scores, normalised to 0–100.
Persona Sensitivity Index
The average absolute difference in SoA between primed (persona-active) and unprimed (baseline) inference trials, across all persona variants tested, normalised to 0–100.
Temporal Stability Score
The average absolute change in core KPI scores (SoA, SR, NGI) between consecutive measurement periods. Lower = more stable. Reported as a normalised index.
Narrative Drift
Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD) between the role/attribute distributions measured in the current period and the prior period. JSD of 0 = identical narrative; JSD of 1 = completely different narrative.
Ch. 5
Action
“What do you actually do with all of this?”
Priority Gap Matrix
A ranked list of identified KPI gaps, scored on two axes: Strategic Impact (how much closing this gap improves brand position) and Effort to Close (estimated investment in content, PR, or partnerships). Gaps are plotted in four quadrants: Quick Wins / Strategic Priorities / Long-term / Deprioritise.
Lever Map
For each gap in the top quadrants of the Priority Gap Matrix, a set of recommended actions specifying: the target channel (PR, owned content, publisher partnership, technical SEO), the specific action, and the KPI it addresses.
Baseline + Target KPIs
For each KPI addressed in the Lever Map, a current baseline score and a target score at 6 months and 12 months, with the expected improvement trajectory stated explicitly.
Quick Win Index
A filtered and ranked subset of the Priority Gap Matrix: gaps in the Quick Win quadrant (high impact, low effort) with an estimated close time of ≤ 90 days. Ranked by impact score descending.
Ch. 1
Demand Landscape
“What does AI believe buyers in this category actually want?”
Decision Driver Ranking
A ranked list of purchase decision criteria extracted from AI responses to open-ended buyer intent prompts, clustered into canonical drivers and ranked by mention frequency across the inference batch.
Category Frame
A structured description of how AI defines the boundaries of the client's product category: included/excluded use cases, included/excluded competitor types, primary framing axis (e.g. price vs. service vs. risk). Extracted from category-definition prompts.
Segment Need Profile
A per-cohort ranking of the top decision drivers, derived by running DDR measurement separately for each cohort definition. Each cohort gets its own ordered need priority list.
Market Narrative Consistency
The degree of agreement across major LLMs on the top-3 decision drivers for a given category and segment, measured as the proportion of driver rankings that are consistent (same top-3, same order) across models.
Ch. 2
Competitive Perception
“How does AI rank and frame competitors in buyers' eyes?”
Competitive Ranking by Decision Driver
For each top decision driver identified in DDR, a ranked list of brands most associated with delivering that driver, derived from competitive association prompts. Each driver gets its own ranking.
Attribute Ownership Map
A matrix of brands × attributes where each cell contains the association strength between brand and attribute, derived from attribute-specific competitive prompts. Normalised so each attribute sums to 100% across brands.
White Space Index
The set of top-N decision drivers (from DDR) where no brand in the competitive set achieves an AOM association score above the dominance threshold (default: 50%). Each qualifying driver is a white space. WSI score = proportion of top-N drivers that are white spaces.
Positioning Sharpness
For each brand, the concentration (Herfindahl-style index) of its AOM attribute ownership — how much its associations are concentrated on a few specific attributes vs. spread evenly across many. Score 0 = perfectly generic; score 1 = fully concentrated on one attribute.
Segment-level Competitive Share
SoA-equivalent presence score computed separately for each brand within each cohort definition, normalised to competitive share within that cohort. Shows how the competitive distribution of AI's responses shifts across segments.
Ch. 3
Segment Intelligence
“How does buyer thinking differ across personas and cohorts?”
Segment Competitive Ranking
A competitive ranking table broken down by cohort: for each cohort, brands ranked by their SCS score. The full cohort × brand matrix of competitive standing.
Cross-Segment Variance
The average pairwise divergence (Jensen-Shannon Distance) between Segment Need Profiles across all cohort pairs. High CSV = segments are substantially different; low CSV = market is relatively homogeneous.
Opportunity Score per Segment
A composite score per cohort combining: (1) Demand Intensity — how strongly expressed are buyer needs in this segment (from SNP frequency scores); (2) Competitive Opening — average white space available to the client in this segment's top drivers; (3) Product Fit — client-declared alignment between their product's strengths and the segment's top drivers. Normalised to 0–100.
Ch. 4
Signal Consistency
“When to trust AI as a research instrument — and when to be careful?”
Cross-Model Consensus Score
The proportion of top-3 decision driver rankings that are consistent across all major models tested, for a given segment and category.
Run-Level Stability
The average overlap (Jaccard similarity) of top-3 decision driver rankings between repeated runs of the same study configuration, across all segments.
Category Validity Score
A composite rating of AI suitability for the category, based on: (1) training data richness signals (CMCS as proxy), (2) response completeness (does AI produce substantive answers or deflect?), (3) category type classification (high-consideration vs. impulse). Rated on a 5-point scale with an associated confidence tier.
Confidence Band per Output KPI
A three-tier confidence label assigned to each output KPI value: High Confidence (CMCS ≥ 70% AND RLS ≥ 0.8 AND CVS ≥ 4), Moderate Confidence (CMCS 50–70% OR RLS 0.6–0.8 OR CVS 3), Directional (CMCS < 50% OR RLS < 0.6 OR CVS ≤ 2).
Instrument Bias Indicators
A set of per-model flags indicating known or detected systematic biases relevant to the study's category and geography, derived from cross-model comparison of findings and a reference bias registry.
Ch. 5
Strategic Implications
“What would you act on — and why does the data justify it?”
Segment Opportunity Ranking
The cohort list sorted by Opportunity Score (OSS) descending, with confidence grade attached to each OSS based on Signal Consistency metrics for that cohort.
Recommended Positioning Statement
A structured positioning statement: [Target segment] who need [top unmet driver], [Client brand] is the [role claim] that [differentiating attribute] because [evidence trace from data]. Generated from the intersection of DDR top drivers, WSI white spaces, and AOM client standing.
Competitive Moves
A per-competitor, per-segment action classification: Attack (client has competitive opening and product fit), Defend (client currently leads but competitor is gaining), Concede (competitor has dominant position client cannot credibly challenge), Monitor (neutral, watch).
Research-to-Action Traceability Map
A structured document listing each recommendation (from RPS, SOR, CM) with its supporting evidence: the specific KPI name, its value, the confidence grade, and the reasoning chain from finding to recommendation.
Confidence-Graded Recommendation Summary
A ranked list of recommendations grouped by confidence tier (High / Moderate / Directional), each with a one-line rationale. Intended as the opening page of the research deliverable.
