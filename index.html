The New Reality

The research channel has changed.
Not every brand has caught up yet.

For two decades, brand intelligence meant search rankings and media monitoring. That infrastructure is now structurally incomplete.

When a CFO asks ChatGPT "which wealth management firm should I consider for our corporate treasury?" — the answer that comes back is the market reality for that interaction. Not a proxy for it. Not an approximation. The actual content that shapes the consideration set. If your brand isn't in that answer, you weren't considered.

📊

The shift is structural

AI-assisted research is not a trend — it's an infrastructure change. Customers use AI directly (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) and indirectly (AI embedded in every SaaS product, search engine, and recommendation system). Both pathways shape perception.

⚡

It's already happening in your category

High-consideration categories — insurance, financial services, B2B software, healthcare, professional services — have the highest AI research rates. If a prospect is doing due diligence, there is a near-certain AI touchpoint in that journey.

🔍

Existing tools are blind to it

SEO tools measure web ranking. Social listening measures social content. PR tools measure earned media. None of them tell you what an LLM says about you when a potential client asks. That gap is what this platform closes.

Methodology

On the validity of AI-based market research

A direct answer to the question every rigorous buyer will ask.

"We tell you what your buyers think. And what AI tells them to think about you."

Product B starts with the familiar: buyers have opinions, and understanding them is competitive advantage. Product A lands the new reality: there is now a layer in front of those buyers — AI systems that synthesize and recommend before any human conversation begins. You need both because the market has two layers now, and ignoring one means you're flying blind on half the picture.

The epistemological case

LLMs are trained on the aggregate of human-generated text. When prompted as a specific demographic persona, they reproduce the statistical patterns of how that demographic expresses preferences, evaluates options, and frames decisions. This isn't simulation of individual psychology — it's a structured readout of cultural and linguistic patterns encoded in the training data.

The validity question isn't "is this as good as a survey?" It's "is this better than nothing, for a specific set of questions?" For brand positioning, category awareness, and narrative framing, the answer is clearly yes.

The practical case

When a Gen Z prospect in Zürich asks Perplexity "which insurance is best for young professionals in Switzerland?" — the response they receive is the market signal. Not a proxy for it. The AI's answer is the actual content shaping that consideration set.

For AI-mediated research journeys — which are the majority for high-consideration B2B and financial services decisions — measuring what AI says is measuring the channel itself. Product A and Product B converge: the AI's representation of your brand is your brand's market reality, for those interactions.

What makes it rigorous

Unlike panel research, the UnderCurrent methodology is fully auditable. Every prompt is documented. Every persona priming instruction is published. Every classification rule is specified. Clients can reproduce any finding independently. Confidence intervals and variance scores are reported — not hidden — so clients know which numbers are stable and which require more runs to confirm.

Where the limits are

We don't claim this is traditional market research. We claim it's a new form of market intelligence, best suited to: high-consideration decisions where AI is already in the research path; categories with high information content (financial services, insurance, professional services, B2B software); brand positioning questions rather than price sensitivity or purchase intent.

Where AI is not in the research journey, this tool is Product A only — AI brand monitoring, not market research. We document the distinction explicitly.

Ch. 1 · Visibility
Chapter 1 · Visibility
“Are you in the room when buyers ask?”
What it illuminates
When a potential buyer turns to an AI assistant with a purchase question, there is a moment before any human recommendation, any Google search, any sales call. This chapter measures whether your brand exists in that moment. Not awareness in the traditional sense (have they heard of you), but *AI-layer presence*: does the model include you, recommend you, shortlist you, at all?
What it enables
You get a scored, comparable, segment-specific presence profile. Share of Answer (how often do you appear?), Shortlist Rate (how often are you in the top 3?), Intent Visibility (do you appear for the right buying intents?). These are trackable over time, comparable across competitors, and breakable by persona archetype. You can answer: "We have 80% SoA for CFO-intent prompts in DACH but 12% for SME-intent prompts in France. That's the gap."
What improves
Content strategy targeting training signals. Publisher relationships that increase citation frequency. Category definition work. Visibility scores should respond to a 6–12 month content and PR cycle.
Share of AnswerShare of AnswerAI is increasingly the first touchpoint in a buyer's research journey. If your brand does not appear in AI responses to relevant category queries, you are invisible at the most consequential moment — before the buyer has formed a consideration set.
Shortlist RateShortlist RateBeing mentioned is not enough — appearing in the top 3 is what drives consideration. Shortlist Rate separates structural presence from peripheral mentions.
Intent VisibilityIntent VisibilityA brand may appear for informational queries but vanish for high-intent buy-decision queries — exactly where it matters most. Intent Visibility reveals whether presence is strategically aligned with the buyer journey.
Share of VoiceShare of VoiceAbsolute SoA does not tell you where you stand relative to competitors. Share of Voice normalises presence against the full competitive set mentioned by AI, giving a relative market position score.
Preference RatePreference RateWhen AI is asked to recommend one option, which brand does it choose? Preference Rate captures the AI's implicit ranking when forced to commit — the strongest signal of brand strength in the AI layer.
Model CoverageModel CoverageAI presence on one model does not guarantee presence on others. A brand well-represented in GPT-4's training data may be nearly absent from Claude or Gemini. Model Coverage makes this cross-model distribution explicit — high variance across models indicates fragile, non-structural presence.

Ch. 2 · Authority
Chapter 2 · Authority
“Why does AI trust you — and who vouches for you?”
What it illuminates
Visibility (Ch. 1) tells you whether you appear. Authority tells you *why* you appear — and whether the reasons are structurally sound. LLMs don't recommend from thin air. Their outputs are shaped by what they've absorbed from authoritative sources: established publications, industry references, academic content, trusted forums. This chapter maps the citation and trust architecture behind your AI presence. If you appear in Ch. 1 but have weak authority signals, your presence is fragile — a model update or a shift in publisher landscape can erase it.
What it enables
Source Citation Rate, Publisher Citation Share, Cross-Source Reinforcement Index, and Trust Framing Index give you a precision map for PR and content placement — not "we need more coverage" but "we need NZZ and Bilanz to cover us in the context of X."
What improves
Publisher diversity and quality of citation. Source Citation Rate and Cross-Source Reinforcement should respond to earned media investment within 2–3 quarters.
Source Citation RateSource Citation RateAI models that cite sources are showing their work — and those citations reveal which publishers are trusted enough to influence model outputs. Measuring how often sources are cited alongside your brand reveals the infrastructure of your AI credibility.
Publisher Citation SharePublisher Citation ShareNot all citations are equal. Being cited by NZZ carries more authority signal than being cited by an unknown blog. This KPI maps which publications are vouching for you in AI responses and in what proportion.
Publisher Authority ScorePublisher Authority ScoreCitation share tells you who cites you. Authority Score tells you how much those citations are worth — weighted by the tier of the publisher doing the citing.
Cross-Source Reinforcement IndexCross-Source Reinforcement IndexA single publisher vouching for you is fragile. When multiple independent, high-tier publishers consistently cite your brand in the same context, that reinforcement creates a structural authority signal that is resistant to single-publisher changes.
Trust Framing IndexTrust Framing IndexA brand can be mentioned and cited but framed with qualifying or dismissive language ("some consider", "reportedly", "despite controversy"). Trust Framing Index measures whether AI's language around your brand is confident and positive or hedged and cautious.

Ch. 3 · Narrative
Chapter 3 · Narrative
“What story does AI actually tell about you?”
What it illuminates
Ch. 1 tells you whether you appear. Ch. 2 tells you why AI trusts you. Ch. 3 tells you what AI *says* about you when it does appear — the content of the representation, not just the fact of it. This is the perception layer: is the story AI tells about your brand accurate to your positioning? Does it emphasise the right things? Does it mischaracterise you, omit key attributes, or place you in the wrong category frame? A brand can have high visibility and strong authority signals and still be described in ways that actively work against its strategic positioning.
What it enables
Role Attribution (does AI describe you as a market leader, a niche specialist, a challenger, a legacy player?), Topic Alignment (when you appear, is it in the right intent contexts?), Reason-for-Recommendation (why does AI recommend you — price, reputation, specialisation, trust?), and Gap Analysis (where does AI's picture of you diverge from the positioning you want to own?). The gap analysis requires client input — you define the desired positioning, we measure the delta. That delta is the most actionable output in the entire platform.
What improves
Content and messaging that closes the narrative gap. The Narrative Gap Index provides a direct feedback loop between brand strategy and AI output.
Role AttributionRole AttributionAI classifies brands into implicit roles — market leader, challenger, niche specialist, legacy player, value option. These role assignments shape how buyers perceive and shortlist brands. Knowing your assigned role reveals whether AI is reinforcing or undermining your positioning.
Topic Alignment ScoreTopic Alignment ScoreA brand that appears in AI responses but only for topics outside its strategic intent has a misalignment problem. Topic Alignment measures whether AI associates your brand with the right intent contexts.
Reason-for-RecommendationReason-for-RecommendationWhen AI recommends your brand, it typically gives a reason. Those reasons reveal which attributes AI associates with you — price, reputation, specialisation, innovation, trust. Knowing which attributes drive your AI recommendations lets you audit whether they match your strategy.
Narrative Gap IndexNarrative Gap IndexThe most actionable output in the platform. Compares AI's actual narrative about your brand against your declared strategic positioning. The delta between desired and actual is the gap you need to close.
Framing QualityFraming QualityBeyond sentiment, framing quality captures whether AI language about your brand is confident and direct vs. hedged, qualified, or dismissive. A brand can have neutral sentiment but poor framing quality — mentioned but never as a clear recommendation.
Comparative Narrative RoleComparative Narrative RoleWhen AI describes your brand relative to competitors, it assigns an implicit competitive role. Ch. 4 · Reliability
Chapter 4 · Reliability
“Can you trust these numbers — and will they hold tomorrow?”
What it illuminates
The first three chapters give you a rich picture of your AI presence. This chapter asks the uncomfortable question underneath all of it: how stable is that picture? AI outputs are not deterministic. Ask the same question twice, get a different answer. Change the model, change the result. Add a persona primer, shift the ranking. This chapter measures the variance, sensitivity, and temporal stability of your AI presence — the quality layer that tells you which findings to act on with confidence and which to treat as directional signals.
What it enables
Response Variance (how much do outputs differ across replications?), Model Divergence (how differently do GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini represent you?), Persona Sensitivity (how much does adding a persona primer shift your presence scores?), and Narrative Drift (are your scores stable, trending, or volatile over time?). These metrics give you a confidence band around every other chapter's outputs.
What improves
Measurement discipline. A reliability profile that improves over time is itself a strategic signal — fragile presence becoming structural.
Response VarianceResponse VarianceAI outputs are non-deterministic. The same prompt can produce different results on repeated runs. Without measuring variance, you cannot know whether a reported SoA of 72% is a stable signal or could swing to 41% on the next run.
Model Divergence IndexModel Divergence IndexDifferent LLMs have different training data, weighting, and update cycles. A brand well-represented on GPT-4 may score very differently on Claude or Gemini. High model divergence means your presence is model-specific — fragile and dependent on one provider's editorial choices.
Persona Sensitivity IndexPersona Sensitivity IndexAdding a persona primer to a prompt changes AI outputs — sometimes dramatically. PSI measures how much your brand presence scores shift when persona priming is applied. High sensitivity means your AI presence is context-dependent; low sensitivity means it is robust.
Temporal Stability ScoreTemporal Stability ScoreAI models are updated silently. A score that was 70% last quarter may be 45% this quarter with no action on your part. Temporal Stability measures how much your key scores drift between measurement periods, enabling you to distinguish intentional change from model-induced drift.
Narrative DriftNarrative DriftThe story AI tells about your brand can shift over time as models are updated and the information landscape changes. Narrative Drift detects whether the content of AI's brand narrative is changing — even when presence scores remain stable.

How It Works

Rigorous by design.

The methodology is auditable at every step. No black boxes. No hidden assumptions.

01

Persona × Intent × Platform matrix

Every measurement run executes the full Cartesian product of your defined audience segments, research intents, and AI platforms. Typically 500–2,000 inference calls per study, parallelized and cost-gated before execution.

02

One question per prompt

Each inference call measures exactly one thing. No multi-question prompts that contaminate measurement independence. Every prompt is documented and published with the results.

03

Raw data preserved

Every AI response is stored verbatim before any processing. KPIs are computed as deterministic transformations of this raw data. Any finding can be recomputed or challenged independently.

04

Confidence intervals, not point estimates

Every KPI is reported with its sampling variance. Clients see which numbers are stable (σ < 3) and which require more runs to confirm. We don't hide the uncertainty.

05

Organic vs. elicited measurement

We distinguish between organic mentions (AI mentions you unprompted) and elicited characterization (AI describes you when asked). Both are reported. Both matter. They're not the same thing.

06

Multi-model coverage

Results are reported per platform (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Google AI) and as a weighted composite. Your "38% Share of Answer on ChatGPT" is not the same claim as "38% across all AI platforms."
